# Case Studies

## Content Checklist (Not a Template)

Section names should describe actual content, not abstract labels (Tania Rascia style).
But every case study must cover these six dimensions — in whatever order and naming makes sense:

1. **Context** — What was the situation? What constraints existed?
2. **Problem** — What needed to be solved? Why did it matter?
3. **Strategy** — What approach was chosen and why? What alternatives were rejected?
4. **Execution** — What was actually done? What decisions were made mid-way?
5. **Result** — What happened? Specific numbers required.
6. **Reflection** — What would be done differently? Be honest.

Example: instead of a section called "Strategy", use "Audience Targeting Approach" or "How We Prioritized".
The goal is that a reader learns how Trevor thinks — not that they see a standard template.

## Case Study 1: Mayora — CNY Meituan Flash Sale Campaign

**File:** `cny-campaign.html` (project deep-dive) + `mayora.html` (full company story)

**One-line summary:** Company's first-ever instant retail campaign, built from scratch with no SOP or historical data. Led RTB advertising across gift box and new product lines.

**Key numbers:**
- Gift box campaign ROI: 7.22 (full CNY window)
- New product sales: ~600% growth vs pre-campaign daily baseline
- Total gift box impressions: 1.38M
- Budget: ¥150K RTB + ¥80K vouchers (¥12K voucher actual spend)
- 5 cities: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Hangzhou, Nanjing

**Strongest decision moment:**
Hypothesis-driven audience targeting — dairy transaction buyers were cut after Week 1 (incremental ROI insufficient), budget shifted to existing customers (ROI 38.7) and gift-giving audience (ROI 7.3).

**Honest gap:**
Should have pre-defined spend ramp criteria instead of relying on intuition. Several new product SKUs weren't live on day one, capping new product line performance.

## Case Study 2: Decathlon — WeChat Mini Program UX Research

**File:** `decathlon-ux.html` (project deep-dive) + `decathlon.html` (full company story)

**One-line summary:** Self-initiated full-funnel UX benchmarking of Decathlon's WeChat Mini Program vs JD 1P/3P. Surfaced 16 pain points, built prioritization framework, drove project approval.

**Key numbers:**
- 16 pain points identified (forward purchase + reverse returns flow)
- 4 reference platforms benchmarked
- 2 alignment meetings with PM to validate quadrant placement
- BRD: delivery radius expansion 3km → 10km, projected ¥6M+ GMV uplift

**Strongest decision moment:**
Pushed back on courier pickup feature (mentor wanted it fast-tracked). Two-angle argument: API integration cost high, return rate spike risk short-term. Feature moved to roadmap, low priority. Mentor agreed.

**Honest gap:**
No internal user behavior data (no heatmaps, no conversion funnel). Used competitive benchmarking as proxy. Would push harder for that data next time.
