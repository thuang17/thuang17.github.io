# Personal Index Direction Design

Date: 2026-05-03
Status: Draft for review

## 1. Direction Shift

The homepage should no longer behave like a polished resume.

It should become a **personal index page**:

- sparse
- deliberate
- high-trust
- low-explanation
- visually restrained

The role of the homepage is not to summarize Trevor.
Its role is to let a visitor choose which part of Trevor to enter first.

The site should feel like a personal website first, and only secondarily function as a professional asset.

This is a deliberate shift away from the previous direction:

> professional case-study site first, personal builder site second

The new direction is:

> personal index first, proof-bearing site second

The homepage becomes the front surface.
Depth lives in the second layer.

## 2. Core Thesis

The site should communicate one thing before it communicates credentials:

> Trevor is becoming someone who can turn ambiguity into motion.

This matters more than role-fit language on the homepage.

The homepage should not immediately optimize for:

- recruiter scanning
- role categorization
- full career summary
- comprehensive self-description

Those can still exist deeper in the site.

The homepage instead optimizes for:

- point of view
- curiosity
- perceived taste
- deliberate entry into the site

## 3. Homepage Role

The homepage should act like an **index card** or **entry surface**, not a reading-heavy landing page.

That means:

- no long-scroll resume structure
- no stacked “experience / projects / skills / contact” homepage sections
- no chapter metaphor
- no explanatory wall of copy
- no attempt to tell the whole story on the first page

Instead, the homepage should contain:

1. Trevor’s name
2. a short thesis-level line or phrase
3. three primary entry points
4. minimal supporting metadata

Everything else moves down the information hierarchy.

## 4. Primary Entry Points

The homepage should foreground exactly three main entry points:

### 1. Work

Purpose:
show the strongest evidence of judgment in real operating environments

Content types:

- Mayora campaign
- Decathlon UX / prioritization work
- future strong work cases only

This is not the full experience archive.
This is selected proof.

### 2. AI / Making

Purpose:
show Trevor as someone who experiments, builds, tests, and shortens the distance between idea and artifact

Content types:

- AI workflow exploration
- personal site building
- tool experiments
- future small builds

This should not be framed as “AI interest.”
It should be framed as evidence of a builder instinct.

### 3. Stories

Purpose:
show the personal material that explains Trevor’s way of working

Content types:

- darts
- future non-work stories with real relevance to discipline, pressure, repetition, or systems

Important boundary:
`Stories` should not become a generic personality bucket.

Current decision:

- `darts` belongs in `Stories`
- `AI` should no longer primarily live in `Stories`

That means the current `ai.html` direction likely needs to evolve later toward `AI / Making`.

## 5. What Moves Out Of The Homepage

The following items should no longer be dominant homepage material:

- full work history
- education block
- skills grid
- contact section as a major homepage destination
- resume-style scanning structure

They can still exist in the site, but not as the homepage’s main skeleton.

Likely destinations:

- secondary info page
- resume/info panel
- lower-priority footer or subpage

The exact placement can be decided during implementation planning.

## 6. Visual Direction

The preferred visual direction is:

- minimal
- cold-to-neutral rather than playful
- precise rather than decorative
- editorial restraint with digital polish

This is closer in spirit to Rauno’s sense of control than to playful maker-portfolio energy.

The homepage should not try to feel:

- cute
- whimsical
- chapter-based
- card-heavy
- warm and chatty

It should feel:

- quiet
- expensive
- controlled
- intentional

## 7. Interaction Direction

The user explicitly rejected animation-heavy entry behavior.

The preferred interaction model is:

- calm at rest
- sophisticated on hover
- precise on click / transition

This means the homepage’s premium feeling should come mainly from:

1. hover behavior on the three primary entry points
2. transition behavior when entering deeper pages

It should not depend mainly on:

- intro animation
- constant motion
- decorative animation loops
- broad page-load spectacle

The static page should already feel restrained.
Interaction should reveal the extra level of precision.

## 8. Information Density

The site should stop trying to “earn depth” by showing more information on the homepage.

The new principle is:

> depth by invitation, not by immediate exposure

That means the homepage should become thinner, not fuller.

This is appropriate for Trevor’s current stage.

Trevor is still early-career.
He does not need to imitate someone with a decade of work and a massive public archive.

The site can feel complete without being content-heavy if:

- the homepage is selective
- the second layer is strong
- the language is confident
- the framing is consistent

## 9. Non-Goals

- Do not rebuild the homepage into a story-heavy longread
- Do not keep refining the current resume-like structure
- Do not use “Selected Proof + Chapters” as the homepage metaphor
- Do not make the homepage explain all parts of Trevor equally
- Do not lead with recruiter-first language
- Do not inject playful or ornamental design just to avoid emptiness

## 10. Success Criteria

The direction is successful if:

- the homepage no longer reads like a resume
- the site feels like a personal website first
- the homepage contains very little, but each item feels deliberate
- visitors understand there are three chosen ways to enter Trevor’s world
- hover and click behavior make the homepage feel more premium without becoming noisy
- work remains credible, but no longer dominates the entire first impression

## 11. Next Design Questions

These are the implementation-facing questions that should be resolved next:

1. What exact thesis line sits under Trevor’s name on the homepage?
2. How should the three primary entry points be worded?
3. What visual form should the homepage take:
   index card, flat index plane, or another restrained surface?
4. What hover language should define premium feel?
5. What transition model should connect homepage to second-layer pages?
6. Where should resume facts live after they move off the homepage core?
